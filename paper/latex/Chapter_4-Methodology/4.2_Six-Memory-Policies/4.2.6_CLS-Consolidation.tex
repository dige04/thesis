\subsection{CLS Consolidation}
\label{sec:cls}

CLS Consolidation forgets by \emph{compression} rather than deletion. On a fixed schedule
--- every $k=5$ tasks (Invariant~\#9) --- it clusters the older memories with DBSCAN, forms
a cluster only where at least three records lie within $0.70$ cosine similarity of one
another, and replaces each qualifying cluster with a single LLM-written summary. This models
the slow neocortical consolidation of Complementary Learning Systems
theory~\cite{mcclelland1995}: stable, repeated structure is abstracted into a compact gist
while one-off details fade. When no cluster qualifies, the policy falls back to Type-Aware
Decay (Section~\ref{sec:type-decay}) at the cap of ten (A1), so it never simply overflows.

The honest finding is that on SWE-Bench-CL this policy is \emph{structurally inert}. The
same-repository memories within a sequence are diverse --- each a different issue --- and do
not form clusters of three or more at the $0.70$ threshold, so consolidation does not fire;
in the gate-3 validation it produced zero consolidation events on both probed sequences
(amendment A3), reducing empirically to its Type-Aware Decay fallback. The matrix-wide
consolidation count is reported with the H3 results
(Section~\ref{sec:h3}) \texttt{[RESULT: pending runs\_144\_seq\_cceb325]}. We deliberately
did \emph{not} relax the $0.70$ threshold to force clustering: that parameter is frozen
(Invariant~\#23), and lowering it to manufacture a positive result would merge dissimilar
memories into incoherent summaries. H3 is therefore reported as a negative,
not-applicable result --- itself a finding about how compressive forgetting behaves on
genuinely heterogeneous coding experience.
