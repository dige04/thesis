\subsection{Recency Prune}

Recency Prune is the simplest content-blind ordering heuristic, one rung above random. When
the store exceeds the cap of ten (A1), it evicts the oldest records first --- lowest
recency --- until the store is back within budget. This is the familiar ``keep what is
fresh'' default, and it uses a single signal, age, to order eviction. Placing it on the
ladder separates a temporal heuristic from both the signal-free random control below it and
the type- and use-aware scoring above it, so the comparison can tell apart recency from
relevance: whether keeping the \emph{newest} records is as good as keeping the records a
content-aware score deems most worth keeping.
